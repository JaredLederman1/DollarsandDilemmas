Core Modules

What We Teach

A step-by-step curriculum that turns abstract money concepts into practical skills teens can use right away.

01

Foundations of Money, Currency & Microeconomics

What money is, why currencies exist, and how supply and demand shape the prices teens see every day — from sneakers to streaming.

  • Money vs. value: what's actually being traded
  • Currencies, inflation, and why a dollar changes over time
  • Real examples of supply, demand, and price changes
02

Earning Money & Planning for the Future

From first jobs and side gigs to long-term goals, students learn to think in trade-offs and make smarter choices.

  • Hourly pay, salaries, and gig work
  • Creating a simple, realistic budget
  • Decision-making and opportunity cost
03

Saving & Growing Money

How small, consistent choices add up over time through savings, compound interest, and the basics of investing.

  • Bank accounts, interest, and safety
  • How compound growth really works
  • Intro to investing: risk, reward, and timelines
04

Spending Money & Financial Tools

How payment tools — from debit cards to apps — actually move money, and what to watch out for with each.

  • Credit vs. debit vs. prepaid cards
  • Payment apps, subscriptions, and hidden fees
  • Building healthy spending habits early
05

Civic Finance & Public Welfare Systems

Connecting personal finance to the bigger picture: taxes, public services, and safety nets that shape everyone's options.

  • Why we pay taxes and what they fund
  • Government programs and public goods
  • The role of insurance and shared risk
06

Financial Safety & Fraud Prevention

How to protect yourself — from online scams and phishing to keeping identity and information safe.

  • Common scams targeted at teens and families
  • How to spot phishing and fake offers
  • Practical steps to protect accounts and identity
